Phase 2 — Automated Refinement Evaluation

Phase 2 takes the Zeb Gardner graded video (confirmed Phase 1 winner) as its input and evaluates a range of subtle automatic adjustments applied on top of the Rec.709 output. The goal is to find a single refinement that consistently improves the Zeb Gardner baseline across all scene types without introducing artifacts, color casts, or loss of highlight/shadow detail.

The pipeline works as follows. A set of candidate adjustments (auto levels, CLAHE, S-curve, saturation boost, cool shadow toning, vibrance) are applied independently to each of the five representative frames selected by the Phase 1 frame analyzer -- shadow, highlight, midtone, motion, and wide_range. Each variant is placed in a contact sheet column alongside the unmodified Zeb Gardner baseline, producing a visual matrix identical in structure to the Phase 1 contact sheet. The contact sheet PNG is then passed to Claude Chat for scored evaluation across four dimensions: color accuracy, highlight preservation, shadow detail, and SE Alaska naturalness. Claude Chat recommends a winner per row and an overall winner across all rows. Gray reviews the contact sheet and Claude Chat's recommendation, confirms or overrides, and the winning adjustment is baked into apply_lut_to_video.py as a permanent Node 3 post-processing step applied after the Zeb Gardner LUT on all future clips.

Phase 2 produces one output: a confirmed Node 3 adjustment that elevates the Zeb Gardner baseline to the target "better than good, professionally shot" quality level for SE Alaska drone footage -- fully automated, no per-clip manual grading required.
